    _Muybridge in Motion_ brings together and animates all 781 plates from Eadward
    Muybridge’s groundbreaking _Animal Locomotion_, a landmark study that broke
    movement into precise photographic sequences. Each plate captures humans and
    animals in motion, walking, running, lifting, and leaping, revealed frame by
    frame with a clarity that reshaped how motion could be seen and studied.
    
    Drawn from multiple institutions, including the National Gallery of Art and several
    Smithsonian museums, these plates are consolidated through the IIIF
    Presentation and Image APIs into unified IIIF Manifests. The result
    demonstrates two ideas. First, that interoperable, open collections allow
    primary sources to be assembled across repositories with clarity and ease.
    Second, that the IIIF Presentation API 3.0 can do more than display static
    images; it can render two-dimensional works as fluid, sequential animations.
