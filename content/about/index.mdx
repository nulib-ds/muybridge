# About

Eadweard Muybridge, a foundational photographer and innovator of moving images, captured _The Horse in Motion_ in 1878. The sequence proved that a galloping horse lifts all four hooves off the ground at once. In July 1887 he published _Animal Locomotion_, a 781-plate collection commissioned by the University of Pennsylvania, though he had made the photographs in 1884 and 1885. Of these, 562 show humans as the primary subject. Plates 563 to 657, around 100 in all, are horses. The best known is _[Annie G.](/search/index.html?q=annie+g&type=work)_, a thoroughbred bay mare captured at a gallop in [plate 626](/works/plate-number-626-annie-g-galloping.html). The remainder capture other animals: livestock such as mules and goats, and wild animals such as elephants, lions, and birds of prey.

## Colophon

_Muybridge in Motion_ is a digital project by [Mat Jordan](https://github.com/mathewjordan) in Academic Innovation at Northwestern University Libraries with the support of leadership, Kelsey Rydland and James Lee. The initial idea came from a conversation with [Trip Kirkpatrick](https://github.com/triplingual) of Yale University Libraries, who first suggested that a cookbook recipe he authored might be used to animate frames of a IIIF resource to showcase the movements captured on flat plates. The project could not be completed as easily without the open-source commitment of [Rainer Simon](https://rainersimon.io/) and his [Annotorious](https://annotorious.dev/) project, which allowed for annotation of the 20,000+ frames of the 781 plates.

The digital project was developed as a customized implementation of [Canopy IIIF](https://canopy-iiif.github.io/app/), a static site generator for IIIF materials. A static workbench was developed on the side to support annotating image frames and updating metadata. All images are provided by their respective institutions: 720 from the National Gallery of Art, 59 from the Smithsonian, and two (2) from Boston Public Library, Digital Commonwealth. Metadata has been extended to include each movement as defined by Muybridge and his colleagues, along with a normalized animal vocabulary. The animations are built using the [IIIF Presentation API](https://iiif.io/api/presentation/3.0/) and rendered using [Clover IIIF](https://github.com/samvera-labs/clover-iiif), developed by Northwestern University Libraries with contributions from other institutions.
