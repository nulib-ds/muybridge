## Colophon

Muybridge in Motion is a digital project by [Mat Jordan](https://github.com/mathewjordan) in Academic Innovation at Northwestern University Libraries with the support of leadership, Kelsey Rydland and James Lee. The initial idea came from a conversation with [Trip Kirkpatrick](https://github.com/triplingual) of Yale University Libraries, who first suggested that a cookbook recipe he authored might be used to animate frames of a IIIF resource to showcase the movement. The project could not be completed as easily without the open-source commitment of [Rainer Simon](https://rainersimon.io/) and his [Annotorious](https://annotorious.dev/) project, which allowed for rapid annotation of the 20,000+ frames of the 781 plates.

The digital project was developed as a customized implementation of Canopy IIIF, a static site generator for IIIF materials. A static workbench was developed on the side to support annotating image frames and updating metadata. All images are provided by their respective institutions: 720 from the National Gallery of Art, 59 from the Smithsonian, and two (2) from Boston Public Library, Digital Commonwealth. Metadata has been extended to include each movement as defined by Muybridge and his colleagues, along with a normalized animal vocabulary. The animations are built using the [IIIF Presentation API](https://iiif.io/api/presentation/3.0/) and rendered using [Clover IIIF](https://github.com/samvera-labs/clover-iiif), developed by Northwestern University Libraries with contributions from other institutions.
